Why they're an Icon

From the OpenTable editors: Open since 1949, this legendary destination is the king of San Francisco steakhouses. From luxurious carved-to-order prime rib rolled out on carts to chefs spinning salads to extra-large martinis with sidecars, every meal in the restaurants old-school dining room decked with ruby-hued booths is rollicking and unforgettable.

                            Business casual. If you want to match the rooms tone for a special night, business casual or nicer is a safe choice.

                            The ambience is classic, oldschool San Francisco, warm, lively and celebratory. Guests repeatedly describe a bustling, clubby dining room with tableside service, lots of energy and often high noise levels. Its perfect for birthdays, anniversaries and festive dinners, but if you want a quiet conversation, request a back room or an earlier weekday seating.

                            The best time is a weekday or an earlier dinner slot when the room is slightly less crowded, midweek evenings often feel calmer. If you prefer the lively, packed atmosphere, weekend nights or later reservations deliver full energy. Whatever you choose, plan far ahead: guests commonly reserve months in advance, and the bar fills quickly for early seating.

                            Busiest: weekend evenings and prime dinner hours, rooms are full, energetic and loud. Because HOPR books out months ahead, offpeak weekday reservations give the most relaxed experience.
